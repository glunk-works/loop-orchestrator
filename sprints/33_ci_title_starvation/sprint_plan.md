### FILEPATH: /sprints/33_ci_title_starvation/sprint_plan.md

**Sprint Goal:** Close **BL-10** — the hole in `.github/workflows/ci.yml` that lets a PR reach an all-green checks page **having never run lint or the test suite**. The planning pass establishes that BL-10 under-states the defect in two ways. First, the trigger is not merely "an over-long title": the permanent-skip mechanism is the `edited` guard, and `edited` fires on **any title or body edit**, including on a PR whose title was always valid — so a second, independent path to the same starvation exists through `concurrency: cancel-in-progress`, which BL-10's preferred fix (drop `needs: pr-title`) does **not** close. Second, the fix is not a new architectural idea: `tests/test_ci_config.py:109` already asserts that a job firing on PR-metadata events must **not** live inside `ci.yml`'s cancelling concurrency group, and `hitl-review.yml` was split out for exactly that reason. `pr-title` is the same case and was simply missed. This sprint therefore **splits `pr-title` into its own workflow**, deletes `edited` from `ci.yml`'s trigger list, and deletes `needs: pr-title` — after which **no job in `ci.yml` carries an `if:` at all, so no job in `ci.yml` can ever report `skipped`.** That is the invariant, and it is what gets pinned by test.

**Out of scope:** **Any change under `src/`.** This is deliberate and load-bearing, not an omission: `hitl-review.yml` filters on `^src/`, so a CI-and-docs-only PR is exempt from the architect-review gate and can land fast — and *every open PR in this repo is exposed to BL-10 until this ships*. The two unfixed findings from PR #39's review (`publish_artifacts` reads every artifact off disk on every stage while both its docstrings claim it "does no I/O"; that read-back uses `Path.read_text()` with no explicit encoding) are **real and still open**, but they touch `src/tools/artifact_store.py` and would drag the fix-to-the-PR-validation-machinery through the very gate it is fixing. They stay in the backlog. Also out of scope: the `pr-title` regex and the 72-char limit itself (the rule is fine — its *coupling* is the bug), any change to `hitl-review.yml`, the five standing checks in `sprints/DEFERRED_VERIFICATION.md`, and any dependency change (so no `sbom` regeneration).

**Context (locked decisions — do not re-open):**

- **FD1 — `pr-title` moves to its own workflow file; the fix is a SPLIT, not a rewiring of `needs:`** *(locked 2026-07-12, Opus/Architect, sprint 33 planning pass; user-confirmed)*. Every guard in `ci.yml` is scar tissue from making one workflow serve two jobs with irreconcilable trigger needs: a **cheap prose check that must re-run on `edited`**, and an **expensive code chain that must not**. The `edited` trigger (L11) exists so a title fix can turn `pr-title` green without a dummy push; the `if: github.event.action != 'edited'` clause (L67) exists so a prose edit doesn't re-run the suite on an unchanged tree; `needs: pr-title` (L65) exists to fail fast. Each is individually correct. Separate the two concerns into two files and **all three delete themselves** — the cheap check keeps `edited`, the heavy chain never sees it, and there is nothing left to gate on. The repo already reached this conclusion once, for `hitl-review.yml`, and wrote the reasoning down as a test.
- **FD2 — There is a SECOND starvation path, through `concurrency`, and BL-10's option (b) does not close it** *(locked 2026-07-12, Opus/Architect)*. `ci.yml` sets `group: ${{ github.workflow }}-${{ github.ref }}` with `cancel-in-progress: true` (L20–22). For a `pull_request` event `github.ref` is `refs/pull/N/merge` — **identical across `opened` and `edited`**. So editing the title *while CI is running* cancels the in-flight run; the replacement run then hits `if: != 'edited'` and skips the heavy chain, and `skipped` is not `failure`. This fires on a PR whose title was **never invalid** and whose suite was **already running green**, so it is not reachable from BL-10's narrative at all. It is closed only by removing `edited` from `ci.yml`'s trigger list (FD1) — dropping `needs: pr-title` alone leaves it wide open, because the killer here is the `edited` guard plus the shared group, not the `needs:`. **This is the reason the minimal fix was rejected.**
- **FD3 — A currently-green test ASSERTS THE DEFECT and must be deleted, not weakened** *(locked 2026-07-12, Opus/Architect)*. `tests/test_ci_config.py::test_lint_job_gates_on_pr_title_to_fail_fast` (L55–64) pins `needs: pr-title` and both `needs.pr-title.result` clauses, with a docstring arguing the fail-fast case. It is not incidental coverage — it was written on purpose to hold this exact structure in place, and it will fail the moment the fix is correct. **A failing test here is the sprint working, not the sprint breaking.** It is replaced (Task 3) by tests for the invariant that actually matters — see FD4 — and must not be softened into a tautology that passes either way.
- **FD4 — The invariant to pin is "no job in `ci.yml` can be skipped", not "the jobs are wired thus"** *(locked 2026-07-12, Opus/Architect)*. Pinning the *new* wiring reproduces the mistake of FD3's test at one remove: it would pass for any future structure that re-introduces a conditional heavy job by a different route. The property that makes BL-10 and FD2 both impossible is stronger and simpler — **after the split, `ci.yml` contains zero `if:` conditions on zero jobs**, and a job with no `if:` and a satisfied `needs:` chain cannot report `skipped`. `pr-title`'s own `if: github.event_name == 'pull_request'` (L29) disappears with the job. Assert the *absence*, structurally (parsed YAML, not substring matching), and BL-10's whole class is closed rather than its one instance.
- **FD5 — The check-run name is the JOB id, so the job id `pr-title` is frozen** *(locked 2026-07-12, Opus/Architect)*. Branch protection matches required checks **by check-run name**, and a check-run is named for the job, not the workflow file. Moving the job to a new file therefore preserves the required check `pr-title` — *provided the job id is unchanged*. Rename it and the required check never arrives, leaving every future PR stuck on an "expected" check forever. The PAT cannot read branch protection (`403 — Resource not accessible`, no `Administration` scope, by design), so this **cannot be verified from here**; it is protected by freezing the id and by a human check (see Risks).

**Security Considerations:** No change to `src/`, to any dependency, to any credential path, or to any of the five sanctioned subprocess surfaces. The change is confined to CI configuration and its tests. It is worth stating the security posture explicitly in the *other* direction, because it is the whole point: today the repo can merge a PR whose **`secrets-scan` (gitleaks) and `dependency-audit` (pip-audit) never ran** — both sit downstream of `lint` via `needs:` and skip with it. BL-10 is therefore not only a test-coverage hole but a **supply-chain and secrets-scanning hole**, and it merges green. This sprint restores the guarantee that those two gates execute on every PR. `pr-title` remains a required check in its own right (FD5) — the title rule is not being relaxed, only decoupled.

**Risks & Blockers:**
- **FD5 is the sharp edge, and it is unverifiable from inside the repo.** If the job id drifts from `pr-title`, branch protection waits forever on a check that no longer exists. **The job id must be copied verbatim.** *(Human action, once the PR is open: confirm in the GitHub UI that the required-checks list still shows `pr-title` and that it reports against the new workflow. Claude cannot read this — the PAT is 403 on branch protection by design.)*
- **YAML parses a bare `on:` key as the boolean `True`.** `on` is a YAML 1.1 truthy, so `yaml.safe_load(ci_yml)` yields `{True: {...}, "jobs": {...}}` — **`cfg["on"]` raises `KeyError`**. Task 3's tests must read `cfg.get("on") or cfg[True]`. This will otherwise cost a confusing debug cycle. Use `yaml.safe_load` (never `yaml.load`; PyYAML is already a dependency and the repo pins this convention in `pyproject.toml`).
- **The fail-fast saving is genuinely being given up, and that is the accepted trade.** A malformed title now burns the full suite's runner minutes instead of stopping at the title check. BL-10 priced this correctly: a few runner minutes against a silently-untested merge. Do not reintroduce a `needs:` to reclaim them.
- **Do not delete `edited` from `pr-title`'s new trigger list.** It is the reason the trigger exists at all — without it a bad title can only be cleared by a dummy push. The bug was never `edited`; it was `edited` reaching the *heavy chain*. Likewise keep `synchronize` and `reopened`: a required check must emit a check-run on **every** head SHA or a pushed commit leaves the PR blocked on a check that never arrives, and `reopened` is what makes the close+reopen recovery work.
- **`ci.yml`'s `concurrency` block stays exactly as it is.** It is correct for code events (a new push should cancel the old run) and `tests/test_ci_config.py:109` asserts `cancel-in-progress: true` is present. Only the *trigger list* changes. The new workflow gets its own group, which cannot collide because `github.workflow` differs.
- **The sprint's own PR runs under its own new config** (for `pull_request` events GitHub reads the workflow files from the PR's merge ref, not the base branch). This is what makes Task 5 possible — and it also means a mistake here is felt immediately, on this PR. Recovery if the PR wedges: **close + reopen** (preserves the head SHA, so a commit-pinned `architect-review` binding survives), never an empty commit.

**Tasks:**

- **Task 1: Extract `pr-title` into its own workflow**
  - **Description:** Create `.github/workflows/pr-title.yml` holding the `pr-title` job, moved **verbatim** from `ci.yml` — same job id (FD5), same regex, same 72-char limit, same error messages. Drop its `if: github.event_name == 'pull_request'` guard (the new workflow only fires on `pull_request`, so the guard is dead). Trigger on `pull_request: types: [opened, edited, reopened, synchronize]` — `edited` so a title fix turns the check green without a dummy push, `synchronize`/`reopened` so a check-run exists on every head SHA (see Risks). Give it its own `concurrency` group following the existing idiom (`${{ github.workflow }}-${{ github.ref }}`, `cancel-in-progress: true`) — it cannot collide with `ci.yml`'s because `github.workflow` differs. Carry the *rationale* comments across, rewritten for their new home: `edited` is load-bearing **here**, and this job deliberately gates nothing.
  - **Target Files:** `.github/workflows/pr-title.yml` (new)
  - **Acceptance Criteria:**
    - The job id is exactly `pr-title` — byte-identical to the id in `ci.yml` today (FD5).
    - The validation step is behaviorally unchanged: same Conventional Commits pattern, same 72-char limit, same `::error::` output.
    - The workflow's trigger types are exactly `[opened, edited, reopened, synchronize]`.
    - The workflow has no `needs:` and no `if:` on the job — it gates nothing and nothing gates it.
    - Lint/format clean; suite still green (Task 3 has not yet inverted the old test, so `test_lint_job_gates_on_pr_title_to_fail_fast` is **expected to still pass here** — `ci.yml` is untouched in this task).

  - **Target Files:** `.github/workflows/ci.yml`
  - **Acceptance Criteria:**

    - The `push: branches: [main, 'feat/**']` trigger and the `concurrency` block are unchanged.
    - The `lint` → `format-check` → `test` → `secrets-scan`/`dependency-audit` → `sbom` `needs:` chain is otherwise unchanged.

- **Task 3: Replace the test that asserted the defect**
  - **Description:** Delete `test_lint_job_gates_on_pr_title_to_fail_fast` (FD3 — it pins the bug; do not weaken it into something that passes either way) and add tests that pin the invariant instead (FD4).
